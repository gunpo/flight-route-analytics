The findings
01 — The delay cascade

Delays don't strike at random.
They pile up through the day.

The first flights of the morning leave on a clean slate. By evening, every aircraft is inheriting the lateness of the flight before it. This is the single most actionable pattern in the data: book the earliest departure you can stand.

—

02 — Route efficiency score

Not all routes are built equal.

I scored every route 0–100 by combining two penalties: excess air time (how much longer the flight takes than its distance predicts) and how often it lands late. Congested corridors into ORD and LGA drag the bottom; long, open runs to the Pacific Northwest top it.

06 — How it works

The pipeline behind the page.

  1. 01

    Ingest

    Reads the US DOT Bureau of Transportation Statistics On-Time Performance schema — the same public dataset airlines and the FAA report against.

  2. 02

    Analyze

    A pandas pipeline builds the route-efficiency score, the hourly delay cascade, carrier in-air recovery, and monthly seasonality.

  3. 03

    Model

    A scikit-learn logistic regression predicts P(arrival delay > 15 min). Weights and the scaler are exported to JSON.

  4. 04

    Deploy

    The static site loads that JSON and runs the model in JavaScript — no server, hosted free on GitHub Pages.
